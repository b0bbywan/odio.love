## What is odio?

Got an amp or a speaker with no smart features — either it never had any, or the manufacturer dropped support years ago? Plug a Raspberry Pi running odio into its input. Suddenly it speaks AirPlay, Spotify Connect, Bluetooth, DLNA, MPD — every modern source, on your network, out of the box. Even a Pi B from 2012 still has a job. Free, self-hosted, no account, no cloud.

You'll want a <abbr title="Digital-to-Analog Converter — turns the Pi's digital audio into a clean analog signal for your amp">DAC</abbr> for a proper listening experience.

**Built for makers first.** The stack runs on a minimal Debian, in a user session, with near-zero system modifications. Out of the box you get a streamer node — minimal surface, nothing locked. Underneath it's still your Debian, and you have the keys — yours to extend, to strip down, to break and fix, to turn into something else entirely.

**Open for developers.** Every node exposes a REST API. The full stack — playback, volume, sources, Bluetooth, services — is programmable over HTTP. No need to dig into the internals to build on top of it.

**Any player, automatically.** The API and UI don't know about specific audio apps. They talk to them through a standard protocol — so anything on the Pi that speaks it shows up on its own. odio ships a curated stack out of the box, but add your own player or remove one you don't use: the API doesn't care, it sees whatever is running.

**The UI replaces your stereo's remote.** Power, volume, play/pause, next, source. That's it. Browsing a Spotify catalog or a 20 000-track local collection stays the job of the Spotify app, BubbleUPnP, myMPD — pick your tool.

Custom clients, custom automations, voice control wired to Piper and Whisper, parental controls your kid will learn to bypass. The API is the product. Your setup, your rules.
